Events

Training Programme on “Computer Forensics” was conducted on 14th April 2016.

The expert from M/s Digital Intelligence, a leader in the field of computer forensics services delivered the lecture and demonstrated the various forensics experiments related to email, mobile and computer disk using Fred Server, a platform for Forensic Recovery of Evidence Devices. The training programme was attended by M.Tech (ISM) Students.

Sabre partners with IGDTUW to incubate software test engineering CoE

Sabre Travel Technologies Pvt. Ltd., is a leading technology provider to the global travel industry, has set up a Quality Assurance Center of Excellence (CoE) at Indira Gandhi Delhi Technical University for Women (IGDTUW) to build an ecosystem of industry-ready test  engineers. Sabre and IGDTUW signed a memorandum of understanding on 29th April 2016 for MCA students only, to set up the knowledge center that will nurture student skills on industry standard test engineering practices through a combined program of workshops and webinars at IGDTUW, Delhi. M/s Sabre has conducted 5-class room session and 3- WebEx sessions at IGDTUW University Campus. A hacker rank test by M/s Sabre travel technologies in September 2016 was held to select students based on their performance for a six months internship program at Sabre’s development centre in Bangalore. Sabre will also provide opportunity to students to undergo ISTQB Training and Certification during the internship. Organizing Team Members: Prof. R. K. Singh(Professor, Dept of IT), Dr. A.K. Mohapatra (Asso. Prof, Dept of IT) and Ms. Nisha Rathee (Asst Prof, Dept of IT)

Training and Certification on Ethical Hacking from EC Council, internationally renowned security certification agency during 28th March 2016 to 1st April 2016

Training was imparted to 14 students of M.Tech (ISM) was organized in the Department by inviting experts from the Industry during 28th March 2016 to 1st April 2016. All the students have successfully qualified the “Certified Ethical Hacking Exam” conducted by EC Council.

Two days International Conference on “Demystifying Global Transformation in Management & Technology” on 29th & 30th April 2016

In the conference, papers on Information Security, Big Data, Cloud Computing and Software Engineering were presented by Researchers. Mr. Pavan Duggal, internationally renowned Cyber Law expert was the Chief Guest of the event.  Mr. Shail Maniar, Vice-President, M/s Sabre Payment Technologies and Head, Sabre Global Development Centre, Bangalore, was the Guest of Honour. Conference sessions were chaired by eminent academicians and experts from industry and R&D institutions.  All the papers presented during the conference were peer reviewed. Proceedings of the conference were published by Cyber Times International Journal.

 

Workshop on Soft Computing Techniques using MATLAB

Workshop on Soft Computing Techniques using MATLAB from 14th June, 2016 to 17th June, 2016, conducted by Dr. Arun Sharma, Associate Professor, Department of Information Technology, IGDTUW organized a Workshop on Soft Computing Techniques using MATLAB from 14th June, 2016 to 17th June, 2016. Total 21 students of B.Tech. (IT) and MCA attended this workshop. Resource person for the workshop was Dr Arun Sharma, Associate Professor – IT. The following aspects were covered during the workshop: 

 1. Introduction to Soft Computing Techniques

 2. Basic Commands of MATLAB

 3. Fuzzy Logic

 4. Artificial Neural Network

 5. Neuro-Fuzzy

Students learned the theoretical concepts of various soft computing approaches and worked on MATLAB commands and programs. Real life class room based case studies were discussed and implemented on Fuzzy, ANN and ANFIS toolbox of MATLAB.  At the end of the workshop, Prof. R K Singh, HoD – IT interacted with the students and motivated them to continue the learning by advising them to work on a real time project by using soft computing technique. A feedback was also conducted for all the students where students were advised to give their suggestions for further improvement. All the participating students will be awarded Workshop Certificates. Dr Arun Sharma announced another Two Days Workshop on Advanced SQL Queries for the students in the month of July.

Workshop on “Developing Proficiency in C Programming”

conducted by Ms. Nidhi Arora , Assistant Professor, IT along with Ms. Ritu Garg, Research Scholar, IT  from 15th June, 2016 to 24th June, 2016. The workshop aimed at providing practical training to the students regarding the basic and advanced concepts of C Programming. Students executed C programs using gcc compiler on LINUX OS and practiced basic set of commands in LINUX. Algorithms on Searching (Linear, Binary search) and sorting techniques (Bubble, Insertion, Selection Sort) were discussed and students performed the hands-on session based on these techniques. Basic concepts of Data Structures such as linked lists, stacks, queues, trees and graphs were also discussed. Students were given advanced case study based programming exercises to be performed in the lab session based on the above mentioned topics.

Workshop on Secure Mobile Development conducted during 30th March 2016 to 16th June 2016 under ISEA Project.

The objective of the workshop was to give hands-on Practical exposure along with strong theoretical concepts of Secure Mobile Application Development. The sessions were conducted by experts from Leading Industry. The students from B.Tech and MCA participated in the Workshop.

A one-week hands on workshop on “PHP Framework for Design and Development of Web Based Applications”

was conducted from 20 th June 2016 to 27th June 2016 under the guidance of Ms. Charu Gupta, Asstt Prof and Ms. Anushree, Research Scholar, Department of IT. Students from B.Tech and  MCA  participated in the workshop. They were taught basics of server side scripting language and advanced level of array and string operation necessary for web app development using a framework based on MVC Architecture.  Students are working on real-life projects.

Workshop on Ethical Hacking was conducted during 20th June to 30th June 2016 under ISEA Project Phase-II.

The participants have been taught various techniques of hacking, penetration testing and cyber forensics. The workshop was conducted under the guidance of Dr. Amar Mohapatra, Associate Professor and the M.Tech (ISM) students who have been certified as Ethical Hackers by Internationally accredited EC Council.

Department of IT awarded with a project grant of 65.06 lacs from DeitY in the last financial year under ISEA project Phase-II

the following labs have been established under the project grant:-

Cyber Forensics Lab : The lab is equipped with FRED Server and Net Force Suite –a forensic suite developed by C-DAC
IoT Lab : The lab has been equipped with real time test bed “SenseNuts: IoT Platform for Excellence” for experimenting sensor for IoT applications.
